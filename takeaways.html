Practical Takeaways for Energy-Aware LLM Quantization

Do not choose quantization by bit-width alone. Benchmark energy directly on the target model, GPU, batch size, and backend.

1. Quantization does not always save energy

Runtime dequantization, mixed-precision outlier handling, kernel scheduling, and backend maturity can offset memory-traffic savings.

2. Benchmark small models carefully

In the evaluated records, 1.1B--1.5B models showed energy overhead under NF4 and INT8.

3. Larger models can benefit

For 6B--9B models, quantization reduced energy use in the available records.

4. The crossover is not universal

Measured brackets and analytical N* values are protocol-specific descriptors, not hardware constants.

5. Backend matters

Fused kernels, AWQ/GPTQ, vLLM-class engines, GGUF paths, and FP8 kernels may behave differently.

6. Do not rank GPUs from these data alone

The study emphasizes within-platform relative changes against FP16 baselines.
